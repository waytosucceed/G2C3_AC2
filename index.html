Gender nouns


Gender nouns are nouns that specify or imply the gender of the person or animal being referred to, such as "actor" (masculine) and "actress" (feminine).

Masculine gender nouns denote male persons or animals, such as "man," "king," and "Lion."

Feminine gender nouns denote female persons or animals, such as "woman," "queen," and "mare."

Neuter gender nouns refer to objects or concepts where gender is not specified or relevant, such as "book," "city," and "tree."
